Course Learning Outcomes

Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, candidates will demonstrate the following competencies:

  1. Critical Analysis & Synthesis: Students will critically analyze and synthesize course readings, assignments, and professional experiences to articulate how educators can best assess and monitor reading and writing progress for advanced, proficient, striving (or struggling) readers/writers, and students with dyslexia, dysgraphia, and other disorders, as well as diverse learners.
  2. Assessment Data Interpretation: Students will interpret a variety of formal and informal assessment data across literacy domains to determine a student's language and literacy competencies and needs and to develop measurable, research-supported learning goals related to both reading and writing.
  3. Instructional Planning & Implementation: Students will plan and implement lessons that incorporate research-supported best practices to meet learning goals and critically reflect on the overall effectiveness of the enacted teaching process.
  4. Communication of Strategies: Students will recommend and communicate instructional strategies and interventions to caregivers and educators based on assessment data.

Alignment with Standards

These outcomes align with the GaDOE Teacher Assessment on Performance Standards (TAPS) and other professional standards.
