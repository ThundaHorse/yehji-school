Course Expectations and Guidelines

Academic Expectations

Preparation and active/positive participation in all discussions and activities is expected. A growth-oriented perspective is essential.

Writing Standards

Students will write in standard American English, adhering to APA (7th ed.) guidelines. All work should be proofread to ensure academic quality.

  • Formatting: Typed, double-spaced, Times New Roman (or similar) size 12 font, 1-inch margins.
  • Citations: Mandatory in-text and bibliographic citations for all sources.

Academic Honesty

All work must be your original work developed this semester. Plagiarism (including ghostwritten papers or uncredited use of others' work) will be dealt with according to the academic misconduct policy. UWG Honor Code.

Generative AI Policy

Permitted Uses

Gen AI tools are permitted only in specific contexts to support learning (e.g., brainstorming strategies, generating assessment examples, draft outlines).

Requirements

  • Review & Revise: AI output must be critically reviewed, revised, and cited.
  • Your Voice: Submitted work should reflect your own voice, writing style, and critical thinking.
  • Citation: Failure to mark, cite, or acknowledge the use of Gen AI tools could constitute plagiarism.

Course Policies

Attendance

Demonstrated by posting in the online discussion during the first week. Students are responsible for verifying their attendance.

Late Work

Late work is not accepted in graduate-level Literacy (READ) courses.

All assignments are due by 11:59 p.m. (Eastern). Extenuating circumstances are considered on a case-by-case basis with required documentation.

Professional Conduct

Students are expected to adhere to all UWG policies, maintain a professional demeanor, collaborate equitably, and treat colleagues/professors with respect.

Communication

  • Tone: Professional and respectful civility in all interactions.
  • Email: Use UWG email. Response time is typically 24-48 hours.
  • Etiquette: Be sensitive, don't use all caps, don't flame, use clear subject lines.

Diversity & Inclusion

It is the intent that students from all diverse backgrounds and perspectives be well-served by this course and that students' learning needs be addressed both in and out of class.
