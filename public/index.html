Literacy Case Study Outline

A Comprehensive Guide for Structuring Your Literacy Case Study
View Case Report (PDF)

Section Overview

  1. Section 1: Student Literacy Biography (Weight: 10%)
  2. Section 2: Assessment Results (Weight: 5%)
  3. Section 3: Intervention Plan (Weight: 10%)
  4. Section 4: Recorded Tutoring Session (Weight: 20%)
  5. Section 5: Recommendations (Weight: 10%)
  6. Section 6: Overall Reflection (Weight: 10%)
  7. References & Appendices (Weight: 5% & 25%)

Detailed Steps & Instructions

Step 1: Preparation & Student Literacy Biography (Section 1)

Goal: Create a detailed picture of the student as a reader and writer.

Key Actions

  • Written Permission: Obtain signed forms from Parent & Student.
  • Questionnaires: Administer Parent Questionnaire and Student Interest Survey.

Content Required

  • Student Interests: Discuss findings from surveys.
  • Assessment Areas: Highlight strengths and areas for improvement in six domains:
    1. Phonemic Awareness
    2. Phonics
    3. Vocabulary
    4. Fluency
    5. Comprehension
    6. Writing

Format: Narrative (at least 2 pages).

Example/Tip:
“Based on the student interest survey, 'Alex' loves graphic novels but avoids chapter books. The parent questionnaire indicates a history of ear infections which may have impacted early phonemic development...”

Step 2: Assessment Results (Section 2)

Goal: Establish a baseline and measure growth.

Key Actions

  • Select Assessments: Choose from the textbook (McAndrews, pp. 355-482) or approved GaDOE list.
  • Testing Schedule: Administer Pre-tests for all 6 literacy domains initially, and Post-tests later in the semester.

Content Required

  • Pretest/Posttest Chart: Display scores side-by-side for comparison.
  • Cloud Links: Provide working links (Google Drive/OneDrive) to the actual scored assessment copies.

Example/Tip:
Create a clear table. Column 1: Domain, Column 2: Assessment Name, Column 3: Pre-test Score, Column 4: Post-test Score.

Step 3: Intervention Plan (Section 3)

Goal: Plan instruction based on data.

Key Actions

  • Analyze Data: Review Pre-test results to identify specific gaps.
  • Create Goals: Develop 3 SMART Goals (Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound):
    • 2 Reading Goals
    • 1 Writing Goal

Content Required

  • Introduction: Brief overview.
  • The Goals: Stated clearly.
  • Rationale: Explanation of "Why this goal?" must include at least one contemporary (2019-present) peer-reviewed research citation per goal.

Format: Narrative (at least 2 pages).

Example/Tip:
“Goal 1: By the end of the semester, the student will increase oral reading fluency from 50 wcpm to 70 wcpm on grade-level text... This goal is supported by Rasinski (2020) who states...”

Step 4: Recorded Tutoring Session (Section 4)

Goal: Reflect on your teaching practice.

Key Actions

  • Record: Film one full tutoring session (ensure meaningful tech use).
  • Review: Watch the video yourself to analyze performance.

Content Required

  • Hyperlink: Working link to the video placed at the start of the section.
  • Reflection: Specific details on what went well, what didn't, and what you would improve. Focus on your teaching, not just student behavior.

Format: Narrative (at least 2 pages).

Step 5: Recommendations (Section 5)

Goal: Provide next steps for stakeholders.

Key Actions

  • Data Analysis: Review Post-test data to inform suggestions.

Content Required

  • For Parents: 3 recommendations in parent-friendly language.
  • For Future Educators: 3 recommendations using professional terminology.
  • Alignment: All recommendations must align with post-test results.

Format: Narrative (at least 2 pages).

Example/Tip:
“For Parents: Continue playing 'rhyming tennis' in the car to build phonological awareness...”

Step 6: Overall Reflection (Section 6)

Goal: Synthesize the entire experience.

Content Required

  • Analysis: Discuss what worked and what didn't work overall.
  • Future Improvements: What would you do differently next time?
  • Citations: Cite a variety of course resources.

Format: Narrative (at least 2 pages).

References & Appendices

Requirements

  • References: APA 7th edition. At least 5 peer-reviewed journals.
  • Appendix A: Scanned Permission Forms (signed).
  • Appendix B: Completed Parent/Student Surveys.
  • Appendix C: Four Tutoring Plans (one must match the video).
  • Appendix D: Four Student Work Samples.
